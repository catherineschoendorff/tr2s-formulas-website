CASE STUDIES

Capital Stewardship in Practice

How European incumbents are preserving asset value and reshaping competitive arenas through regenerative transformation.

Why 2019 → 2024?

This five-year window captures a CEO's realistic transformation horizon.

2019: Pre-COVID baseline, before the EU Green Deal

2024: Post-pandemic execution, regulatory clarity

Five years is sufficient for a CEO to demonstrate strategic impact on asset configuration and capital allocation.

Understanding the Metrics

TR²S and TR³S extend traditional shareholder return measurement.

TR²S and TR³S are financial metrics - not ESG scores, not sustainability ratings. They quantify how asset preservation and ecosystem restoration translate into adjusted shareholder returns.

Consumer Goods & Fashion

Pandora A/S

Denmark
PNDORA.CO

Stock Performance - IPO to Today

Key Price Movements

2018-2019 Crisis and turnaround: Stock fell from DKK 900+ to DKK 255 amid brand fatigue. New CEO Alexander Lacik launched "Programme NOW" restructuring.
2021 Lab-grown diamond commitment: Pandora became the first major jeweller to use only lab-grown diamonds, signalling circular positioning.
2024 100% recycled materials achieved: Full transition to recycled gold and silver. Stock peaked at DKK 1,317. ROIC reached 46%.
2025 Macro headwinds: Weak consumer sentiment in North America and Europe. Rising silver prices and FX headwinds pressured margins. Stock declined to ~DKK 500.
2026 Strategic transition: New CEO Berta de Pablos-Barbier. Shift to platinum-plated "Evershine" collection to reduce commodity exposure. Guidance: -1% to -2% organic growth.

1 case

Signify N.V. (Philips Lighting)

Netherlands
LIGHT.AS

Stock Performance - IPO to Today

Investment Thesis

  • Light-as-a-Service (LaaS) pioneer with 35% circular revenue
  • 10-year contracts with performance guarantees, 144M light points
  • Recurring revenue model with design-for-disassembly architecture

Core Financial Gates

ROIC 7.23% vs WACC 7.1% (+13 bps, marginal)
FCF EUR 438M (7.1% sales), down 25% YoY
Net Debt 1.3x EBITDA, strong balance sheet

Key Price Movements

2016 Philips spin-off: Signify (then Philips Lighting) IPO at EUR 20. World's largest lighting company begins independent journey.
2019 LaaS model scaling: Light-as-a-Service reaches 25% of revenue. Stock climbs to EUR 27.86. ROIC above 20%.
2021 Peak performance: Stock reaches EUR 50+ amid COVID-driven home renovation boom and smart lighting demand.
2023-2024 Market headwinds: China softness, European weakness, professional lighting decline. Stock falls to EUR 21. CEO departure announced Jan 2025.
2025-2026 Circular model validated: 35% circular revenue, 144M light points under service contracts. Awaiting market recovery.

Transformation Story (2019 → 2024)

Signify's transformation from 2019 (EUR 20.49) to 2024 (EUR 21.16) demonstrates how regenerative business models moderate downside risk: TRS -25.16% cushioned to TR²S -15.75% through strengthened circular operations (RM 0.835 → 0.935, circular revenue 25% → 35%).

Light-as-a-Service model transformation under market headwinds.

Construction & Built Environment

1 case

Sika AG

Switzerland
SIKA.SW

Stock Performance — IPO to Today

Key Price Movements

2019 Parex acquisition: Sika acquires mortar specialist Parex for CHF 1.7bn, expanding into facades and tile adhesives. Revenue grows +14.4%. Stock rises from CHF 124.60 to CHF 181.85.
2023–2024 MBCC Group integration: Completion of the MBCC acquisition (construction chemicals). Record revenue CHF 11.8bn, EBITDA margin 19.3%. Synergies in adhesives and concrete admixtures.
2024 Bolt-on acquisitions & market expansion: Kwik Bond (US refurbishment), Vinaldom (Caribbean), Chema (Peru). Cross-selling and geographic diversification accelerate.
2024–2025 Market headwinds: Weak construction and automotive markets in China and Europe. Organic growth 1.1%, FX impact −2.7%. Stock declines from CHF 258.90 to CHF 215.80. Share gains maintained despite volume pressure.
2025 Dividend increase: Board proposes +9.1% dividend increase to CHF 3.60, signalling confidence in long-term capital generation despite cyclical headwinds.

Transformation Story (2019 → 2024)

Sika's transformation from 2019 (CHF 124.60) to 2024 demonstrates how disciplined capital stewardship through acquisitions and asset integrity management can sustain value creation even under severe market headwinds. Revenue grew +45% from CHF 8.1bn to CHF 11.8bn, EBITDA improved +11%, and ROIC held at 14.2–22.1% despite a difficult construction cycle. The 2024 TRS of −15.1% reflects cyclical market conditions, not structural deterioration — a distinction the TR²S framework is designed to capture.

CEO Thomas Hasler (since 2021) executing Strategy 2028: 6–9% local currency growth, EBITDA margin target 20–23%.

  • 2023–2024: MBCC Group integration completed — largest acquisition in Sika history
  • 2024: reCO2ver® technology deployed — CO₂ binding in concrete, clinker reduction up to 50%
  • 2024: Scope 1+2 emissions reduced −10.3% vs. 2023; GHG reduction target −20% by 2028
  • 2025: Dividend +9.1% — signal of balance sheet confidence despite cyclical pressure

Investment Thesis

  • Global market leader: 11% market share, 102 countries, fragmented market (Top-10: 40%)
  • EBITDA margin 19.3% (target 20–23%), ROIC 14.2–22.1% consistently above WACC
  • reCO2ver® circular concrete technology — enabler for EU climate targets and US IIJA
  • Acquisition-led growth (Parex, MBCC) with proven integration track record

Core Financial Gates (2024)

ROIC 14.2% vs WACC 8.84% (+534 bps spread; adjusted 22.1% / +1,326 bps)
FCF CHF 1,384M (11.8% of revenue), positive for 10+ consecutive years
Net Debt / EBITDA 2.0x (well below 3.0x threshold)

Board Relevance: Full Proceed. Sika passes all three capital gates with strong ROIC spread (+534 bps), consistent FCF generation (11.8% of revenue), and conservative leverage (2.0x). The 2024 TRS of −15.1% reflects cyclical construction market weakness, not structural impairment. Asset integrity is intact: MBCC integration complete, reCO2ver® deployed, dividend raised +9.1%. The board should apply TR²S to distinguish CEO capital stewardship from market cycle noise.

Renault SA — Re-Factory Flins

France
RNO.PA

Stock Performance — All Time

Key Price Movements

2018–2019 Diesel transition crisis & Ghosn affair: Renault faces profitability pressure from diesel phase-out and the arrest of CEO Carlos Ghosn. Stock falls from €90+ to €53.82 (Jan 2019). TRS −15.03% for 2019 as restructuring begins.
2020 COVID & strategic reset: Renault posts record losses of €8bn. New CEO Luca de Meo appointed. “Révolution” strategic plan launched — refocus on profitability over volume. Stock hits decade lows below €20.
2021 Re-Factory Flins launched: Renault converts the historic Flins plant into Europe’s first circular economy factory dedicated to remanufacturing, repair, and reuse of vehicles and components. A structural bet on the circular economy at industrial scale.
2023 Ampere EV spin-off & recovery: Renault creates Ampere (EV & software division) and Horse (combustion engines JV). Group returns to profitability: operating margin 7.9%, net income €2.3bn. Stock recovers to €40+.
2024 Flins scaled to 6,000 motors/month: EV transition completed at Flins. Battery remanufacturing reaches 3,000 units/year. Circular revenue target set at €2.3bn by 2030. TRS +32.89%, TR³S +58.08% reflecting circular value retention.
2025–2026 Market headwinds & tariff pressure: European EV demand softens, US tariff uncertainty, Chinese OEM competition intensifies. Renault leverages circular operations as structural cost advantage. Stock trades around €32–35.

Transformation Story (2019 → 2024)

Renault Flins’ transformation from 2019 crisis (€42.18) to 2024 recovery (€47.05) demonstrates how circular economy infrastructure creates structural resilience: +32.89% stock appreciation, Re-Factory scaled to 6,000 motors/month, and TR³S outperformance (+58.08%) reflecting value retention through remanufacturing. The 2019 negative TRS (−15.03%) was cushioned by TR²S (−12.77%) and TR³S (−10.85%), demonstrating downside protection even before the circular model was fully operational.

CEO Luca de Meo (since 2020) executing “Révolution” plan: profitability over volume, circular economy as structural competitive advantage against Chinese OEMs.

  • 2021: Re-Factory Flins launched — Europe’s first circular economy factory at industrial scale
  • 2023: Ampere EV spin-off created; operating margin restored to 7.9%
  • 2024: Flins scaled to 6,000 motors/month; battery remanufacturing 3,000 units/year
  • Target: €2.3bn circular revenue by 2030 at 10%+ margin

Investment Thesis

  • Circular economy factory at industrial scale — first mover in automotive remanufacturing
  • 6,000 motors/month, 3,000 batteries/year remanufactured at Flins
  • €2.3bn circular revenue target by 2030 (10%+ margin)
  • Structural cost advantage vs. Chinese OEMs through asset reuse

Core Financial Gates (2024)

ROIC 3.77–4.15% vs WACC 2.35% (+1.5–2.0 pp, limited margin)
FCF growth positive, reinvested in circular capacity
Group net cash €7.1bn — strong balance sheet

Board Relevance: Conditional Proceed. Renault meets minimum thresholds with limited margin. ROIC (3.77–4.15%) barely exceeds WACC (2.35%) by 1.5–2.0 pp. FCF trajectory and Flins-specific leverage remain to be monitored. Key opportunity: circular economy as structural advantage vs. Chinese OEMs. Key risk: Renault Group execution risk, market adoption of remanufactured components, and financial sustainability of Flins operations. TR³S (+58.08% in 2024) demonstrates the board should apply the extended metric to capture circular value creation not visible in TRS alone.

Rolls-Royce Holdings plc

United Kingdom
RR.L

Stock Performance — All Time

Key Price Movements

2019 Trent 1000 durability crisis: Rolls-Royce faces GBP 2.4bn in compensation costs for Trent 1000 engine blade failures. Stock falls from 281.67p (Jan 2019) to 234.45p (Dec 2019). TRS −15.90% as the company absorbs the full financial impact of the crisis.
2020–2022 COVID-19 aviation crisis: Global aviation collapses. Rolls-Royce, paid by flying hours under TotalCare LTSA contracts, faces existential cash pressure. Emergency rights issue raises £2bn. Stock crashes below 50p. TotalCare contracts provide structural stability — revenue deferred, not lost.
2023 CEO Tufan Erginbilgic appointed: New CEO launches radical turnaround — “burning platform” speech. Operational efficiency programme targets £400–500M savings. mtu Reman Technology Centres scale to 95% content recovery. Stock recovers strongly through the year.
2024 Aerospace demand rebound & TotalCare expansion: Civil aerospace flying hours recover to pre-COVID levels. TotalCare LTSA contracts expand. FCF reaches £2.1–2.2bn. ROIC 12.74–15.88% vs WACC 11–14%. TRS +91.49%, TR³S +335.45% reflecting full circular infrastructure value.
2025–2026 Narrow-body gap & supply chain constraints: Rolls-Royce’s wide-body strength (85% of fleet) contrasts with limited narrow-body exposure. Supply chain bottlenecks constrain engine delivery. Stock trades around 1,200–1,300p. Monitor: aerospace cycle volatility and narrow-body market gap.

Transformation Story (2019 → 2024)

Rolls-Royce’s transformation from 2019 crisis (234.45p) to 2024 recovery (568.60p) demonstrates circular infrastructure advantage: +91.49% TRS, but TR²S +175.20% and TR³S +335.45% show regenerative metrics outperform market timing. The TotalCare Power-as-a-Service model — where customers pay per flying hour — creates structural lock-in and predictable long-term cash flows. mtu Reman Technology Centres achieve 95% content recovery, transforming end-of-life engines into new components at a fraction of manufacturing cost.

CEO Tufan Erginbilgic (since 2023) executing radical turnaround: operational efficiency, TotalCare expansion, and circular supply chain as structural competitive moat.

  • 2019: Trent 1000 durability crisis, GBP 2.4bn compensation, TRS −15.90%
  • 2020–2022: COVID-19 aviation crisis — TotalCare LTSA contracts provide structural stability
  • 2023: CEO Tufan Erginbilgic appointed, operational turnaround begins
  • 2024: Aerospace demand rebounds, TotalCare expansion, mtu Reman scales to 95% recovery

Investment Thesis

  • TotalCare Power-as-a-Service contracts transferring maintenance risk to RR — structural customer lock-in
  • Remanufactured engines (95% content recovery) via mtu Reman Technology Centres
  • Circular supply chain: ~50% recovered materials remanufactured as new components
  • Pricing power through long-term LTSA contracts — revenue visibility 10–20 years

Core Financial Gates (2024)

ROIC 12.74–15.88% vs WACC 11–14% (0–5 pp spread)
FCF £2.1–2.2bn (2024) from LTSA contracts
Balance sheet: 0.0–0.5x Net Debt/EBITDA (net cash, BBB/A-2)

Board Relevance: Full Proceed. Rolls-Royce passes all three financial gates. TotalCare model provides pricing power through long-term customer lock-in. Remanufacturing creates structural competitive advantage. TR³S (+335.45% in 2024) dramatically outperforms TRS (+91.49%), demonstrating that circular infrastructure value is massively underweighted in standard return metrics. Monitor: aerospace cycle volatility, supply chain constraints, narrow-body market gap (85% of fleet is wide-body).
