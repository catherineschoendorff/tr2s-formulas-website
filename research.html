PhD Research

Research on
Extended Shareholder Return

Closing the gap between CEO tenure and transformation horizons. A capital stewardship framework for boards.

The Vision-Execution Paradox

CEOs recognise the need for systemic transformation. Boards judge performance before those transformations mature. Average CEO tenure has fallen to 7.1 years; an increasing number of mandates are compressed to 3 to 3.5 years. Total Shareholder Return tells boards how the market responded. It says nothing about whether the firm built or eroded the asset base that will generate future returns.

This research develops and tests a framework that captures the capital stewardship dimension of shareholder value creation. The central question: Can this dimension be measured with financial methods that boards and reward committees can apply?

Scope

The formulas apply to any business model: regenerative, linear, extractive, or transitional. They measure the gap between market return and long-term stewardship cost. The framework does not favour one model over another. If the logic proves sound, it may inform analytical tools, investor disclosures, or board-level governance mechanisms.

This work is academic research. It does not provide investment advice, strategic guidance, or endorsement of any business model. The formulas and case applications are exploratory. They test a thesis.
