PhD Research

Research on
Extended Shareholder Return

How do CEOs of European incumbent firms embed regenerative circularity at the normative-strategic level to sustain competitive advantage.

Purpose and scope

This research develops and tests a return framework that captures the capital stewardship dimension of shareholder value creation. It answers a specific question: Can this dimension be measured with financial methods?

The approach extends Total Return to Shareholders (TRS) to include the long-term cost of asset stewardship. This reflects an incumbent firm's obligation to preserve the resource base that sustains its business.

What this research does and does not do

The formulas apply to any business model: regenerative, linear, extractive, or transitional. They measure the same thing: the gap between market return and long-term stewardship cost.

The framework does not favour one model over another.

The cases included serve an illustrative and directional role.

They show how the return mechanics behave when applied to real companies operating under different strategic and asset configurations.

The calculations:

  • do not distinguish between a company's prior state and subsequent transformation steps
  • do not assess whether current patterns are intentional or transitional
  • read the current configuration as given

Each case answers a narrow question:

Given this company's current financial and operational parameters, what does the return look like under the extended framework?

Cases were selected based on data accessibility, sector diversity, and strategic clarity. All inputs derive from publicly available filings or well-documented estimates.

Where inputs are estimated, this is stated explicitly. The goal is transparency, not precision.

A positive Extended TRS figure means the company's market return exceeds its long-term stewardship cost. A negative figure means the opposite.

These figures are not performance ratings. They do not imply sustainability merit or strategic failure. They express a financial relationship between return and cost under a specific measurement lens.

From research to application

This framework is intended as a contribution to capital stewardship measurement. It is not ready for direct adoption in valuation or governance decisions.

If the logic proves sound, it may inform future analytical tools, investor disclosures, or policy mechanisms. That is the intended direction, not the current state.

Closing clarification

This work is academic research. It does not provide investment advice, strategic guidance, or endorsement of any business model.

The formulas and case applications are exploratory. They are designed to test a thesis, not to recommend action.
